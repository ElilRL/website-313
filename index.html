Supervolcano Beneath Your Feet:
Yellowstone sits atop one of the world's largest active volcanic systems. Its last major eruption was 640,000 years ago—it was 1,000 times more powerful than Mount St. Helens!
This famous geyser erupts roughly every 90 minutes, shooting 3,700-8,400 gallons of boiling water up to 185 feet in the air.
Three Cataclysmic Eruptions:
2.1 million years ago (Huckleberry Ridge eruption) - 2,500x more powerful than Mt. St. Helens.
1.3 million years ago (Mesa Falls eruption) - Smaller but still devastating.
640,000 years ago (Lava Creek eruption) - Created the current caldera and blanketed much of North America in ash.
Wildlife & Ecosystems
America's Serengeti: Yellowstone is home to the largest concentration of mammals in the lower 48 states, including grizzly bears, wolves, and herds of bison and elk.
The park has about 4,900 bison—some of the last purebred wild bison in the U.S.—and they often cause "bison jams" by walking on roads.
Wolves were reintroduced in 1995 after being wiped out in the 1920s. Today, about 100 wolves roam Yellowstone, helping balance the ecosystem.
